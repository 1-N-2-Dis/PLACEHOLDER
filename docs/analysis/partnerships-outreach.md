## 1. How to read this — the exchange

For every org, think in three columns:
- **What they give us:** interview participants, credibility/endorsement, distribution to women, a
  funding/GAD path, domain guidance (so we handle sensitive data right), or just a quotable "we're in
  conversation."
- **What we offer them:** an advocacy-aligned tool built *with* their community; anonymized,
  aggregate *conditions* insight (never crime labels, BR-001) they can fold into their own GAD
  Accomplishment Reports or advocacy; a student-built demonstration that RA 11313 (Safe Spaces Act)
  can be operationalized; co-branding on a pilot.
- **How they enter the pitch:** as *evidence* (the problem is real + on-theme), as *traction* (we've
  reached out), or as the *model* (institution-funded). Keep these three uses distinct.

## 2. Tier A — reachable & warm (act on these in the next 3 days)

These are the only ones that can realistically do anything before July 9. Prioritize them.

### A1. PUP's own Gender and Development Focal Point System (GFPS) — **customer #1**
- **What it is:** every State University (PUP included) is legally required to run a GAD Focal Point
  System and allocate a GAD budget (Magna Carta of Women, RA 9710; PCW–DBM–NEDA joint guidance).
  This is the office that already has money and a mandate for exactly this.
- **Verification:** ✅ the *mandate* is confirmed (all SUCs have a GFPS). ⚠️ **the exact PUP office
  name + contact is NOT confirmed here** — likely under the VP for Research/Extension, the Office of
  Student Affairs, or a dedicated PUP GAD office. **Team: confirm on campus this week** (ask the
  Guidance & Counseling Office or OSA "who runs GAD here and how do I reach them"). Do not guess a
  name in the pitch.
- **They give us:** the funding path (GAD budget), institutional credibility, a legitimate "we've
  opened a conversation with PUP GAD" traction line, and possibly interview participants.
- **We offer:** a tool their own students built that advances their RA 11313 mandate, plus anonymized
  conditions insight for their GAD Accomplishment Report. The one-paragraph 6-week pilot ask is
  already drafted in the session handoff (§4) — reuse it verbatim.
- **Pitch use:** the **payer/model beat** ([pitch kit §3, 3:15–4:15](./alex-pitch-kit.md)) and the
  "who pays / is it sustainable?" Q&A row. If you get *any* reply, it upgrades to a traction line.

### A2. GDG on Campus – PUP (the SparkFest organizer itself)
- **What it is:** your own in-network host. They know the team and the project already.
- **Verification:** ✅ (they're running the event; Ate Gianne of GDG already advised you).
- **They give us:** warm introductions to PUP orgs / GAD, amplification of the recruiting post, and
  credibility-by-association. Fastest warm door you have.
- **We offer:** a strong showcase project from their own hackathon; a case study they can point to.
- **Pitch use:** not a slide — a *connector*. Ask them this week to point you to the right PUP GAD
  contact and to boost the interview post.

### A3. PUP student orgs & college student councils (esp. women/gender/social-science orgs)
- **What it is:** section groups, college councils, and any campus women's collective, sociology/
  psychology/political-science orgs whose members commute the zone.
- **Verification:** ⚠️ specific org names/pages not confirmed here — **find them via your own network
  + GDG PUP**. They exist; identify the real current ones before messaging.
- **They give us:** the fastest, warmest **interview participants** (Channel B in the interview
  guide) and distribution to more women commuters.
- **We offer:** a project for their community; co-credit; a reason to engage their members.
- **Pitch use:** feeds the **validation** story ("we recruited interviewees through PUP orgs"), not a
  partnership claim.

### A4. Manila City GAD Office / Women's Desk + Sta. Mesa barangay VAW desks
- **What it is:** Manila LGU has a GAD office; every barangay has a mandated VAW desk (RA 9262). PUP
  Sta. Mesa sits inside specific Manila barangays.
- **Verification:** ✅ the *mandate* is confirmed (RA 9262 requires barangay VAW desks;
  [PCW](https://pcw.gov.ph/faq-republic-act-no-11313/)). ⚠️ specific desk officers/contacts not
  confirmed — identify the exact Sta. Mesa barangays and their desks locally.
- **Caveat:** VAW desks are oriented to domestic violence (VAWC), not commute harassment — a

  channel.
- **They give us:** the LGU-GAD side of the payer model (beyond PUP), local legitimacy.
- **We offer:** anonymized conditions insight relevant to their jurisdiction; an RA 11313 demonstrator.
- **Pitch use:** the **"can it grow / who else pays"** answer — LGU GAD desks after PUP.

## 3. Tier B — real, national, but slow (use as citations & precedent, not partners)

These are heavyweight and unreachable-for-a-deal in 3 days. Their value is **evidence and
legitimacy** in the pitch, plus a real post-hackathon path. Cite them; don't claim them.

### B1. UN Women — Safe Cities Metro Manila *(strongest citation you have)*
- **What it is:** UN Women's real, donor-funded flagship program on women's safety in Metro Manila
  public spaces — explicitly public transport, terminals, and walking home. It began in Quezon City,
  and UN Women even ran a **hackathon for mobile-tech solutions to street harassment** in Metro
  Manila. ([UN Women Safe Cities Metro Manila](https://asiapacific.unwomen.org/en/countries/philippines/safe-cities-metro-manila);
  [tech hackathon](https://asiapacific.unwomen.org/en/news-and-events/stories/2016/06/using-technology-to-address-sexual-harassment-of-women-in-public-places))
- **Why it's gold:** it validates your *entire premise* from an unimpeachable source — and their own
  data reports street-harassment prevalence rising to **88% for women aged 18–24**
  ([UN Women](https://asiapacific.unwomen.org/en/news-and-events/stories/2016/03/un-women-and-qc-government-safe-cities-progamme)),
  which *is your exact segment*. Content rephrased for licensing compliance.
- **Pitch use:** **problem beat + Relevance (theme).** One line: "UN Women's own Safe Cities Metro
  Manila data finds street-harassment prevalence reaches 88% for women 18–24 — our exact users." This
  is a stronger validation asset than any seed pin, and it's on-theme (public safety, institutions).
- **Verification:** ✅ program + stat confirmed via UN Women pages above.

### B2. Philippine Commission on Women (PCW)
- **What it is:** the national government machinery for women; the authority behind the **Safe Spaces
  Act (RA 11313, "Bawal Bastos")** and GAD budget policy.
  ([PCW on RA 11313](https://pcw.gov.ph/faq-republic-act-no-11313/))
- **Pitch use:** the **"why now"** legal anchor (RA 11313 makes commute/public-space harassment a
  named, penalized priority — already in [pitch kit §3](./alex-pitch-kit.md)) and the legal backing
  for the GAD-budget payer model.
- **Verification:** ✅. Reachable by email/FB for the record, but do not expect a 3-day reply.

### B3. Plan International Philippines — "Safer Cities for Girls" / SAFE Online (Manila)
- **What it is:** INGO running girls'-urban-safety and online-safety programs, including in Manila.
  ([Safer Cities for Girls](https://plan-international.org/protection-from-violence/safer-cities-girls/);
  [Plan PH](https://plan-international.org/philippines/))
- **Caveat:** their Safer Cities focus is **ages 13–18**, younger than your 18–24 — an *adjacent*, not
  exact, fit. Cite as precedent that "safer cities for women/girls" is a funded, serious field.
- **Pitch use:** precedent + a post-hackathon funder/partner lead. Not a July-9 partner.
- **Verification:** ✅ programs confirmed.

### B4. GABRIELA (national alliance of women's organizations)
- **What it is:** long-standing national women's org/movement, Manila-rooted, focused on women's
  rights and VAW. ([Gabriela Women's Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabriela_Women's_Party))
- **Honest caveat:** it's also a national-democratic political party-list — **politically colored.**
  For an institution-funded (PUP/LGU-GAD) pitch, leaning on Gabriela could complicate the neutral,
  government-aligned framing. Useful as a *women's-community connector* for interviews/distribution;
  be deliberate about foregrounding it in a pitch to institutional judges.
- **Verification:** ✅ org confirmed; ⚠️ current local chapter contact not confirmed here.

## 4. Reaching out — method & templates

Same discipline as the [interview guide](./user-interview-guide.md) and Ate Gianne's advice: **lead
with learning and alignment, not a sell.** Private, professional-conversational, specific. Never open
by pitching the app.

**Sequencing (do this order):**
1. **Today:** GDG PUP contact (A2) → ask for the right PUP GAD contact + a post boost. Your own
   network + PUP orgs (A3) → book interviews.
2. **This week:** once GDG/your network confirms the real PUP GAD office (A1), send the org email
   below. Optionally PCW/UN Women/Plan for the record (B) — low expectation, pure upside.

**Template — org / GAD email (adapt; confirm the real recipient first):**

> Subject: PUP student project on safer commutes for women — seeking your guidance
>
> Good day [Office/Name], I'm [Alex], a PUP student. My team is building **GuidHer**, a student
> project that helps women commuters around PUP Sta. Mesa see *conditions* on their route — lighting,
> how busy a stretch is, recent community reports — **before** they travel. It's aligned with the
> Safe Spaces Act (RA 11313) and PUP's own GAD mandate.
>
> We're not selling anything. We'd value 15–20 minutes to **learn from your work** and understand how
> a tool like this could responsibly support women students. If helpful, we can share what we learn
> from our user conversations (anonymized, aggregate — no personal data).
>
> Would a short call or message thread this week be possible? Thank you for the work you do.
> — [Alex], [contact]

**Template — women's org / student org DM (warmer, shorter):**

> Hi po! PUP students kami, gumagawa ng project para sa ligtas na commute ng kababaihan sa Sta. Mesa
> (aligned sa Safe Spaces Act). Hindi po kami nagbebenta — gusto lang po matuto sa karanasan ng inyong
> community, at kung pwede, makahanap ng ilang women commuters na pwedeng ma-interview (15 mins,
> private, anonymous). Open po ba kayo sa maikling usapan this week? Salamat po! 💜

**Rules:** confirm the recipient is real and current before sending; keep it about *their* work and
*learning*; promise anonymized/aggregate data only (BR-001, no crime labels); never promise the app
rescues anyone (BR-002).

## 6. Inserting orgs into the pitch — exact placement

Map to the beats already in [pitch kit §3](./alex-pitch-kit.md) and the Q&A map in §4. Keep the three
uses separate and **honest** — overclaiming a partnership is a fast way to lose the room in Q&A.

1. **Problem beat (0:00–0:30):** open with the UN Women 88%-for-18–24 stat (B1). It's the strongest,
   most on-theme validation line you have — and it's *their* segment.
2. **Why-now beat (2:15–3:15):** RA 11313 / PCW (B2) as the legal mandate that makes this urgent (this
   is already in the script — now it's sourced).
3. **Model/payer beat (3:15–4:15):** PUP GAD (A1) as customer #1, LGU-GAD (A4) as the growth path.
   Only say "we've opened a conversation with PUP's GAD office" **if you actually emailed and it's
   true** — even an auto-reply or "let's talk after exams" makes it a legitimate traction line.
4. **Q&A rows** ([pitch kit §4](./alex-pitch-kit.md)):
   - "Have you validated the problem?" → UN Women data + your fresh interview quotes.
   - "Who pays / is it sustainable?" → PUP GAD mandate + the drafted pilot ask.
   - "Isn't this a crime map?" → unchanged (BR-001); orgs don't change this answer.

**What NOT to say:** "We're partnered with UN Women / PCW / Plan International." You are *aligned with*
and *citing* them. The distinction is the difference between credible and caught-out.

## 7. Honest bottom line

- **In 3 days, the win is not a partnership — it's:** (a) 2–3 org citations wired into the pitch
  (UN Women, PCW — free, do it today), (b) interviews recruited through GDG PUP + your network + PUP
  orgs, and (c) *one* real "we've opened a conversation with PUP GAD" line if you send that email now.
- **The real partnership work is post-July-9.** PUP GAD and LGU-GAD are the genuine payer path; UN
  Women / Plan International are post-hackathon funder/credibility leads. Don't spend scarce pre-pitch
  hours chasing a signature that can't happen in time.
- **Do not fabricate a contact or a commitment.** A cited public stat is bulletproof; a claimed
  partnership you can't back is a Q&A landmine. This is the same anti-hallucination rule the whole
  repo runs on ([POSTMORTEM](../POSTMORTEM.md)).
