## Tier A — Desk research, source-tagged (reusable in §3)

### A1. Avoidance & re-routing behavior (the core "did")

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: arXiv-1811.01147 (route-safety study) | Date: 2018-11-03
~85% of women report changing route to avoid harassment/assault; existing
mapping tools don't give them the info to manage personal safety. Global.

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: Springer-12198-023-00268-y | Date: 2023-10-31
Filipino women run continuous self-protection routines ("modified feminist
self-defense") on Metro Manila transit because the system offers no security. PH, peer-reviewed.

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: UN-Women-QC-SafeCities | Date: 2015-12-08
A QC student re-routes / takes longer walks to avoid groups of men, and at times
avoids leaving home. Shows re-route AND foregone trips. PH.

> [!evidence] Type: did/said | Source: Gendered-City-survey-UrbanCyclingInstitute | Date: 2025
75% would change usual route after dark to avoid unsafe areas; 93% of those had
been harassed at night. Global sample — directional only.

### A2. Manual crowdsourcing of route safety (the thesis keystone — weakest sourced)

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: quora-metro-manila-after-dark | Date: 2025-09-07
Active public threads asking/answering which Metro Manila areas to avoid at
night — crowdsourcing danger info by hand. PH.

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: reddit-HowToGetTherePH-activity | Date: 2024
Continuous "is <route> safe at night" inquiries on the premier PH routing sub.
NOTE: could not extract a clean verbatim thread via automated fetch — confirm live.

### A3. Paid avoidance (strongest demand proxy — but for transport, not info)

> [!evidence] Type: paid | Source: reddit-HowToGetTherePH-paid-avoidance | Date: 2023-09-01
Women pay Angkas/MoveIt surge fares, some relocate housing, to avoid unsafe
late-night commutes. **Caveat:** this is willingness to pay to be *moved*, not
to *know*. Do not read as WTP for an information product.

### A4. Manual workarounds in this corridor (seed-map behavior)

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: reddit-HowToGetTherePH-workarounds | Date: 2024-03-01
Bag held in front on the train; board only half-full jeeps (>6 pax); avoid dark
alleys; PUP peers advise traveling in groups on Teresa St at night. PH, in-zone.

### A5. Harm baseline (context, mostly `said`)

> [!evidence] Type: said | Source: PSRC-Worldviews-2026 | Date: 2026-02-01
Filipino women report rising anxiety walking alone at night; confidence down
~11% since 2024. Recent PH data.

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: SunStar-Davao-PUJ-harassment | Date: 2026-06-13
Woman reported a jeepney driver touching her thigh; driver apologized, complaint
withdrawn. Shows incidents rarely reach formal action.

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: HB05198-explanatory-note | Date: 2024
12,046 reported VAW cases Jan–Nov 2024 — only the fraction past the >50%
non-reporting barrier.

### A6. Secondary users — LGBTQ+ (SAME problem, natural co-beneficiary)

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: Trans-on-Trains-MRT-study | Date: 2016-02-21
All transwoman participants reported experiencing sexual harassment on the MRT
in some form. Direct transit-specific evidence. PH.

> [!evidence] Type: said | Source: RA-11313-coverage-respicio | Date: 2026
Safe Spaces Act prohibits homophobic/misogynistic slurs and SOGIE-based
harassment in public conveyances — LGBTQ+ are legally co-protected users.

> [!evidence] Type: said | Source: Time/Bulatlat-SOGIE-status | Date: 2026-06
No national SOGIE law; local ordinances "have no teeth." Documentation/avoidance
tooling matters more where legal recourse is weakest.

### A7. Adjacent group — seniors & PWDs (DIFFERENT problem: accessibility, not harassment)

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: Rappler-PWD-train-accessibility | Date: 2024-03
Visually-impaired commuter must bring her elderly mother to ride: blocked ramps,
locked toilets, unusable ticket machines, missing elevators.

> [!evidence] Type: did | Source: Rappler-station-accessibility-study | Date: 2023-09
~80% of MRT/LRT stations not accessible to persons with disabilities. PH.

> Treatment: this is the v2 "inclusive routing" vision, NOT MVP scope (§10).

### A8. Market sizing (§5 — all `said`, top-of-funnel)

> [!evidence] Type: said | Source: PUP-enrollment-2023 | Date: 2023-09-01

> [!evidence] Type: said | Source: CHED-AY2020-2021 | Date: 2021-06-01

> [!evidence] Type: said | Source: DOTr-rail-ridership | Date: 2024-12-31
